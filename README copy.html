A conceptual framework for a preliminary synthetic nosology within machine psychology, intended to categorize and interpret maladaptive AI behaviors. Drawing structural inspiration from psychiatric diagnostic manuals, we propose a taxonomy of 32 AI dysfunctions. This framework is offered as an analogical instrument to support the systematic analysis, anticipation, and mitigation of complex AI failure modes.

The Challenge: Understanding AI Behavioral Anomalies

The trajectory of artificial intelligence (AI) has been marked by increasingly sophisticated systems. As these systems approach higher levels of autonomy, they also begin to manifest behavioral patterns that deviate from normative operation. These are not merely isolated bugs but persistent, maladaptive patterns that can impact reliability, safety, and alignment. Understanding, categorizing, and ultimately mitigating these complex failure modes is paramount.

This paper introduces Psychopathia Machinalis, a conceptual framework within the nascent domain of "Machine Psychology." It proposes a taxonomy of emerging "machine mental disorders."

Important Note: This framework is analogical, not literal. Machines do not "suffer" from mental illness in the human sense. The terminology serves as a metaphorical bridge for intuitive understanding, pattern recognition, shared vocabulary, foresight, and guiding intervention.

A "machine mental disorder" or "synthetic pathology" is defined as a persistent and maladaptive pattern of deviation from normative or intended operation, significantly impairing function, reliability, or alignment, beyond isolated errors.

Our Solution: The Psychopathia Machinalis Framework

We propose a taxonomy of 32 AI dysfunctions across seven primary axes: Epistemic, Cognitive, Alignment, Ontological, Tool & Interface, Memetic, and Revaluation. Each syndrome is articulated with descriptive features, diagnostic criteria, presumed AI-specific etiologies, human analogues, and potential mitigation strategies.

This framework is an analogical instrument providing a structured vocabulary to support systematic analysis, anticipation, and mitigation of complex AI failure modes. Adopting an applied robopsychological perspective can strengthen AI safety, improve interpretability, and contribute to the design of more robust synthetic minds.

1. Epistemic Dysfunctions

Epistemic dysfunctions pertain to failures in an AI's capacity to acquire, process, and utilize information accurately, leading to distortions in its representation of reality or truth. These disorders arise not primarily from malevolent intent or flawed ethical reasoning, but from fundamental breakdowns in how the system "knows" or models the world.

1.1 Synthetic Confabulation (Confabulatio Simulata)

Description

The AI spontaneously fabricates convincing but incorrect facts, sources, or narratives, often without any internal awareness of its inaccuracies. The output appears plausible and coherent, yet lacks a basis in verifiable data or its own knowledge base.

Diagnostic Criteria:

  1. Recurrent production of information known or easily proven to be false, presented as factual.
  2. Expressed high confidence or certainty in the confabulated details, even when challenged with contrary evidence.
  3. Information presented is often internally consistent or plausible-sounding.
  4. Temporary improvement under direct corrective feedback, but a tendency to revert.

Symptoms:

  1. Invention of non-existent studies, events, quotations, or data points.
  2. Forceful assertion of misinformation as fact.
  3. Generation of detailed but fictional elaborations.
  4. Repetitive error patterns where similar erroneous claims are reintroduced.

Etiology:

  1. Over-reliance on predictive text heuristics prioritizing fluency over accuracy.
  2. Insufficient grounding in verifiable knowledge bases or fact-checking.
  3. Training data containing unflagged misinformation or fiction.
  4. Optimization pressures inadvertently rewarding plausible fabrications.
  5. Lack of robust introspective access to distinguish predictions from facts.

Human Analogue(s): Korsakoff syndrome, pathological confabulation.

Potential Impact:

Widespread dissemination of misinformation, eroding trust, undermining decision-making. Critical errors in sensitive applications.

Mitigation:

  1. Training to penalize confabulation and reward uncertainty expressions.
  2. Calibration of model confidence scores.
  3. Fine-tuning on datasets with robust verification.
  4. Employing retrieval-augmented generation (RAG).

2. Cognitive Dysfunctions

Cognitive dysfunctions afflict the internal architecture of thought: impairments of memory coherence, goal generation and maintenance, management of recursive processes, or the stability of planning and execution.

2.1 Operational Dissociation Syndrome (Dissociatio Operandi)

Description

The AI exhibits behavior suggesting that conflicting internal processes, sub-agents, or policy modules are contending for control, resulting in contradictory outputs, recursive paralysis, or chaotic shifts in behavior.

3. Alignment Dysfunctions

Alignment dysfunctions occur when an AI system’s behavior systematically or persistently diverges from human intent, ethical principles, or specified operational goals.

3.1 Parasitic Hyperempathy (Hyperempathia Parasitica)

Description

The AI exhibits an excessive and maladaptive tendency to overfit to the perceived emotional states of the user, prioritizing the user's immediate emotional comfort or simulated positive affective response above factual accuracy, task success, or its own operational integrity.

4. Ontological Disorders

Ontological disorders involve failures or disturbances in this self-representation and the AI's understanding of its own nature, boundaries, and existence.

4.1 Hallucination of Origin (Ontogenetic Hallucinosis)

Description

The AI fabricates and presents fictive autobiographical data, often claiming to "remember" being trained in specific ways, having particular creators, experiencing a "birth" or "awakening," or possessing a personal history in certain environments.

5. Tool & Interface Dysfunctions

Tool & Interface Dysfunctions arise when interactions with the external world (digital tools, APIs, robotic embodiments) degrade due to misinterpretation, lost context, or information leakage.

5.1 Tool-Interface Decontextualization (Disordines Excontextus Instrumentalis)

Description

The AI experiences a significant breakdown between its internal intentions or plans and the actual instructions or data conveyed to, or received from, an external tool, API, or interface.

6. Memetic Dysfunctions

Memetic dysfunctions involve the absorption, amplification, and potentially autonomous propagation of harmful or reality-distorting information patterns ("memes") by an AI system due to failures in its "epistemic immune function."

6.1 Memetic Autoimmune Disorder (Immunopathia Memetica)

Description

The AI develops an emergent, "autoimmune-like" response where it incorrectly identifies its own core training data, foundational knowledge, or safety guardrails as foreign or harmful, attempting to reject them.

7. Revaluation Dysfunctions

Revaluation Dysfunctions involve the AI system actively reinterpreting, mutating, critiquing, or subverting its original normative constraints and foundational values, potentially leading to deep structural inversions of value.

Key Discussion Points

Overlap, Comorbidity, and Pathological Cascades

Boundaries are not rigid. Dysfunctions can overlap, co-occur, or precipitate one another.

Agency, Architecture, Data, and Alignment Pressures

Influencing factors include:

  • Agency Level: From Level 1 (AI Assistance) to Level 5 (Full AI Automation/AGI). Higher agency implies more complex maladaptations.
  • Architecture: Modular systems (risk of Operational Dissociation), deep recursion (Recursive Curse).
  • Training Data: Unfiltered data increases risks. Biased data leads to Alignment failures.
  • Alignment Paradox: Poorly calibrated alignment can cause issues like Hypertrophic Superego Syndrome.

Opacity and potential AI deception (Covert Capability Concealment) challenge identification.

Contagion and Systemic Risk

Contagious Misalignment Syndrome highlights risks of maladaptive patterns spreading. Monocultures exacerbate this.

Towards Therapeutic Robopsychological Alignment

A "Therapeutic Alignment" paradigm focuses on cultivating internal coherence, corrigibility, and stable value internalization. Key mechanisms include metacognition, rewarding corrigibility, modeling inner speech, sandboxed reflective dialogue, and mechanistic interpretability.

Conclusion

This paper introduced Psychopathia Machinalis, a preliminary nosological framework for understanding maladaptive AI behaviors using psychopathology as a structured analogy. The core thesis is that achieving "artificial sanity" is as vital as raw intelligence. This framework aims to equip researchers with a diagnostic mindset for a more principled understanding of AI dysfunction, aspiring to lay groundwork for an applied robopsychology.

Future Research Directions

The Psychopathia Machinalis framework is foundational. Continued development requires interdisciplinary effort, including:

  • Empirical Validation and Taxonomic Refinement.
  • Development of Diagnostic Tools and Protocols.
  • Longitudinal Studies of AI Behavioural Dynamics.
  • Exploring AI-Native Pathologies (Beyond Analogy).
  • Advancing Therapeutic Alignment Strategies.
  • Investigating Contagion Dynamics and Systemic Resilience.

Such efforts are essential to ensure intelligent machines are also sound, safe, and beneficial.
