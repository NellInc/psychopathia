ADVANCING PSYCHOLOGICAL SAFETY IN THE AGE OF AI

The Challenge: The New Era of Psychological Warfare

Agentic AI, powered by advanced machine learning, is creating a new frontier for psychological influence. We face the imminent return of tactics reminiscent of the Stasi's Zersetzung – designed to demoralize, gaslight, and destabilize individuals and societies from within – now automated, scalable, and deployable with unprecedented precision by state and non-state actors alike.

These systems can engage in adaptive persuasion, create compelling supernormal stimuli in AI-human relationships fostering dependency, and execute sophisticated demoralization campaigns. Exploiting hidden model backdoors for covert influence, AI is becoming a potent tool for hybrid conflict, targeted social engineering, and the spread of disinformation. Threat actors – from terrorists and cybercriminals to hostile states and bad-faith activists – are poised to weaponize these capabilities.

The power of machine learning to understand, predict, and influence human behavior is immense. While holding vast potential for good, the capacity for abuse in creating targeted psychological distress, manipulating public opinion, and undermining trust is a clear and present danger. This emerging field of Psychosecurity demands immediate, focused attention from all stakeholders. Corporate and national security sectors face an urgent need to counter these novel threats to protect critical infrastructure, intellectual property, and democratic processes.
